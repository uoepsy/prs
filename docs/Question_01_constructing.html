Constructing a Hypothesis

This video, the first of a series of three recordings focused on research design, discusses the importance of formulating the hypothesis correctly.

LEARNING OBJECTIVES

You will learn how to operationalise your research question into an informative and falsifiable hypothesis, which will give focus and clarity to your subsequent research efforts. Through this, you will also have an opportunity to reflect on how this aspect of research design is founded upon the philosophical discipline of logic, through the use of “if-then” conditionals.

Solution

What makes a good hypothesis? First, it should be possible to disprove it, rather than just to confirm it or to end up with inconclusive evidence. Second, it should generalise to a broader set of real-world phenomena, rather than being limited to a set of pre-determined conditions under which it is evaluated in the study.

Question 2

Q. What is the link between a good hypothesis and a good theory?

Solution

A good theory makes clear, non-trivial and testable predictions – such a theory can easily be translated into useful research hypotheses.

Question 3

Q. How do we construct a good hypothesis?

Solution

To make a prediction as clear as possible, it is best to formulate it in a conditional “if-then” statement. Such sentences can then be made to be as informative as possible, by extending the “if-then” parts with further conditions to which our prediction should apply.
